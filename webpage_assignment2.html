Founders of html and css

1. Tim Berners-Lee

Born in 1955, Sir Tim Berners-Lee is a British computer scientist and MIT professor. In addition to his contributions to HTML, he developed the HTTP (Hypertext Transfer Protocol) and URIs (Uniform Resource Identifiers) that form the foundation of the World Wide Web. He has been a strong advocate for open web standards and is involved in various initiatives aimed at ensuring a free and open internet.As the inventor of the World Wide Web, Tim Berners-Lee has been recognized with numerous awards and honors for his groundbreaking contributions. He has actively advocated for net neutrality, privacy protection, and the open sharing of information. Berners-Lee has also been involved in projects like the Web Foundation, which aims to ensure the web is a public good accessible to all.

2. Håkon Wium Lie

Håkon Wium Lie, born on July 15, 1965, in Oslo, Norway, is a prominent figure in the world of web development and standards. He is best known as one of the co-creators of CSS (Cascading Style Sheets), a language used to describe the presentation of web pages. Lie's involvement in CSS began in the early 1990s when he was working as a graduate student at the University of Oslo. Alongside Bert Bos, another web pioneer, he co-authored the first draft of the CSS specification in 1994. Their collaboration resulted in a groundbreaking separation between the structure and presentation of web content, enabling more efficient and flexible web design. Håkon Wium Lie has played a pivotal role in advocating for CSS as a fundamental technology for web development. His work has focused on refining CSS standards, making them more accessible, consistent, and powerful. Lie has actively contributed to web standards organizations, including the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), which oversees the development and maintenance of CSS specifications.
